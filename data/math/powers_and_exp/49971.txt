Problem:
Compute
$e^\left(
\begin{array}{cccc}
 2 & 3 & 3 & 0 \\
 -4 & -5 & -4 & 1 \\
 4 & 4 & 3 & -1 \\
 4 & 4 & 4 & -2 \\
\end{array}
\right)$.
Answer:
$\left(
\begin{array}{cccc}
 e^2 & \frac{e^3-1}{e} & \frac{e^3-1}{e} & 0 \\
 \frac{1-e^4}{e^2} & \frac{1+e-e^4}{e^2} & \frac{1-e^4}{e^2} & \frac{e-1}{e^2} \\
 \frac{e^4-1}{e^2} & \frac{e^4-1}{e^2} & \frac{-1+e+e^4}{e^2} & \frac{1-e}{e^2} \\
 \frac{e^4-1}{e^2} & \frac{e^4-1}{e^2} & \frac{e^4-1}{e^2} & \frac{1}{e^2} \\
\end{array}
\right)$
